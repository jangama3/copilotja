Certification Types

Student Pilot Certificate – A student pilot certificate gives authentication for you to
take flight instruction from a licensed instructor. This is the first part toward earning a
P.P.L.. To obtain a Student Pilot Certificate, allowing you to pursue flight training, you need to
meet basic eligibility requirements. First, you must be 16 years of age. (If you intend
to pilot a glider or balloon, you must be 14 years old.) As well, you must be able to
proficiently read, speak, and understand English. English has been designated as
the universal language in aviation.

Private Pilot License – The most common type of pilot license issued
by the FAA. In order to gain your FAA private pilot license you must log a minimum of 35 hours of
verified flight time while passing the written tests, pass the FAA check-ride, and contain a valid drivers license.
An F.A.A. P.P.L. certificate allows you to fly in most single-engine airplanes and aircraft,
some additional instrument rating tests may be required for more advanced aircraft or if
you wish to be rated to fly under Instrument Flight Rules (I.F.R.). With a PPL, you are authorized
to fly alone or with other people, but you may not receive compensation for flying or taking people
on flights.

The Airline Transport Pilot License - Becoming a commercial airline pilot in the United States
or becoming a commercial airline pilot outside of the
United States you need to complete the first two pilot licenses (P.P.L. and C.P.L.) before you can earn your Airline Transport Pilot certification (A.T.P.).
To earn your commercial airline transport pilot license, you must meet following FAA ATP requirements. You must be at
least 23 years old, have a valid drivers license, pass all exams and tests, pass the flight exams,
log more than 1500 hours of flight time (in the U.S.) in various weather conditions and in numerous
types of aircraft, pass the I.R. courses and ground school courses, pass all medical and eye exams,
and be of sound body, mind and execution.
